# CarterCo SendSpark video — speed-to-lead demo

The personalised video the cold email links to. One premise, proven live on the
prospect's own funnel: **Louis signs up as a lead, nobody follows up, and that
silence is the pitch.** The video doesn't *describe* speed-to-lead — it *is* a
speed-to-lead failure, captured on camera.

This is the asset referenced in the bucket cold email (see
[outreach-playbook.md](outreach-playbook.md) for the engagement loop that fires
once they watch it).

## Premise

A few days before recording, Louis submits the prospect's own lead form
(demo request, contact form, "get in touch" — whatever inbound path they run).
By recording time they still haven't responded. The video opens on that fact.

The proof is first-hand and true: he genuinely signed up, they genuinely went
quiet. It's an operator observation, not a performative trust-grab — the whole
point is that it actually happened.

## Beat-by-beat

1. **The hook (first ~15 sec).** "Hej Thomas — jeg skrev mig op som lead hos jer
   for et par dage siden, og jeg har ikke hørt fra jer endnu." Said plainly, no
   gotcha tone. Screen-share the timestamp of the signup if it lands cleanly.
2. **Why it matters.** Tie it back to *their* world: rising demand means more
   inbound, and the first vendor to respond usually wins the deal. A few days of
   silence is a lead cooling to a competitor.
3. **The fix / the win.** What a fast response would have done — and that this is
   exactly what Carter & Co builds and runs: the lead comes in, the follow-up
   goes out in minutes, not days. Keep it concrete, not a feature list.
4. **Soft close.** No hard CTA in the video — the reply happens over email/LinkedIn.
   End on "tænkte det var værd at vise dig."

## Truth-check against the email copy

The email line and the video frame **must agree**. The video shows a *problem*
(they ghosted a real lead). So the email opener has to set that expectation:

- ✅ "...faldt over et åbenlyst hul" / "et hurtigt fix" — matches a problem-reveal video.
- ⚠️ "...en hurtig gevinst" — only honest if the video *closes on the solution*
  (beat 3 carries real weight), so the click pays off as a win, not just a callout.

Don't promise a "gevinst" and deliver only a "you lost me as a lead" — that reads
as bait-and-switch the moment they click. Pick the frame, then make the video
earn it. (See the no-fabricated-proof and walk-the-funnel-before-promises rules.)

## Per-prospect fill-ins

| Slot | Source |
|------|--------|
| Greeting name | prospect first name |
| Which form he signed up to | their actual inbound path — verify it exists before recording |
| Days of silence | real elapsed time since signup |
| "Why it matters" hook | tie to a real signal (their LinkedIn comment, hiring, demand growth) |

## Worked example — Biogasclean / Thomas

- **Signal:** Thomas commented on a REGATEC 2026 post about increasing market
  demand for complete gas-treatment solutions (desulphurisation, methanation,
  upgrading, polishing).
- **Email opener** ties his demand comment to the speed-to-lead bottleneck, then
  links the video.
- **Video:** Louis signed up as a Biogasclean lead a couple of days earlier, no
  response — demonstrating that the rising demand he's excited about is leaking at
  the follow-up stage.
- **Frame:** because the video is a problem-reveal, the email should say "hul" /
  "fix" rather than "gevinst" unless beat 3 closes hard on the solution.
